Frontend Developers: Not Graphic Designers, But Sometimes We Still Spill Some Blue Milk

As a front-end developer, your main focus is on coding, functionality, and creating smooth user experiences. But let's be honest—sometimes, the job calls for a little more than just JavaScript and CSS Suddenly, you’re expected to add a graphic, tweak an image, or decide if a colour scheme “feels right.” We’re not graphic designers (and we don’t pretend to be), but basic design skills can be incredibly helpful. You don’t need to be the next Photoshop wizard, but understanding colour theory, typography, and layout can make a big difference in the final product.

Of course, this doesn’t mean you won’t spill a little blue milk along the way. Picture this: You’re tasked with creating an icon in Illustrator, thinking you’ve nailed it, only to realize the final product looks like it belongs in a child’s drawing book. It’s frustrating, yes—but it’s also part of the process. I’m guilty of this myself, and that’s exactly why I ended up drawing a spilled carton of blue milk as my personal “design disaster.” (Hey, at least I got some Illustrator practice in!) It’s the kind of moment that reminds us that even with some design knowledge, we’re still bound to make mistakes. And that’s perfectly okay.

The real benefit of having basic design skills, though, is the ability to move faster, collaborate more effectively, and create a better product overall. Sure, it might not always be flawless, but being able to handle minor design tasks means you don’t have to wait for someone else to fix every little detail. So, embrace the spills, learn from the mistakes, and remember: As front-end developers, we may not be graphic designers, but we sure do know how to clean up a mess when we need to.

Why Brand Voice and Design Are a Match Made in Digital Marketing Heaven

When you’re building a brand, especially for a young, creative audience, every detail counts—from the tone of your words to the colours, images and shapes that surround them. It's all about creating an experience that feels cohesive, authentic, and memorable. Take the landing page for a made-up productivity app for digital agencies and creators, for example. The call to action—“Make your hustle count and manage your digital projects easily”—is dynamic, approachable, and speaks directly to the hustle culture of digital agencies. But if the design didn’t match that energy, the message wouldn’t hit as effectively. Here’s where design comes in: I’ve chosen a soft background and a warm, neutral tone that evokes a sense of calm and approachability. It’s not too overwhelming, allowing the more vibrant elements to stand out. This gives a sense of balance and clarity—something important when you’re dealing with busy professionals who need simplicity and focus.

To infuse personality and energy into the page, I've added organic, off-circle shapes in rich purple and bright orange. These aren’t overpowering; they’re placed strategically, creating an inviting, slightly whimsical feel without crowding the space. The purple conveys creativity and depth, while the orange brings warmth and enthusiasm. It’s not just about adding colour for the sake of it—it’s about infusing the design with life that mirrors the message we’re sharing: get things done, but do it with a little flair.

And the best part? The balance of negative space. The careful use of space around these colours and shapes allows each element to breathe, maintaining a sense of organization and flow. It's about keeping things simple and effective—letting the design enhance the message, not compete with it.

So, why does this matter? Because a brand’s voice and design are two sides of the same coin. They need to complement each other, not just coexist. When your messaging and design are in sync, you create an experience that feels whole—one that resonates deeply with your audience. It’s the difference between a forgettable interaction and a memorable, impactful one.

What’s your take on the relationship between brand voice and design? How do you ensure that they work together seamlessly?

The Joys and Struggles of Integrating Forms with CRM and Productivity Apps: A Love-Hate Relationship

Have you ever spent hours, cups of coffee, and countless moments of pure determination trying to integrate a form into a CRM or productivity app , only to hit that dreaded “Submit” button and see… nothing? Or worse, see a vague error message that sends you spiralling into the abyss of API documentation?

Ah, the joy of tech integration.
We all know the feeling. It starts off with optimism: “How hard can it be?” You find a nice form builder, link it to your CRM, and bam, you’re ready to collect data like a pro. But then, reality sets in. API docs are longer than a Tolstoy novel. Syntax errors appear when you least expect them. And even though everything seems perfect, something — somehow — always goes wrong.

This is the world of integrating forms, folks. It requires patience, a ton of trial and error, and, of course, more coffee than most doctors would recommend.

Let me share a story. I was recently working on a form that needed to be integrated into ClickUp. I meticulously filled out the form with the following info:

Name: Is Here
Company Name: You Better Work Email: youbetterwork@gmail.com Message: My fingers are crossed!
I clicked “Submit,” fingers trembling, hoping for smooth sailing. But instead? A sea of errors. The kind of errors that make you question your career choices. The kind that makes you want to crawl into a blanket fort and pretend everything is fine. But of course, the only way forward is more coffee. And more patience. And maybe a little swearing under your breath.

But here's the thing: when it finally works, when that form finally integrates seamlessly with your CRM there is a sense of triumph. Like conquering a mountain, only to discover it was just a molehill that got way too much credit.

The key takeaway: Integrating forms into CRMs and productivity tools may be a rollercoaster of frustrations, but the satisfaction when it all clicks into place? Totally worth it. So, to all of you brave souls who’ve spent hours reading through API docs, making endless tweaks, and enjoying the sweet nectar of coffee... this post is for you. You’re not alone in this!

Keep pushing forward. It’s all worth it in the end — even if it takes a few (hundred) attempts..
